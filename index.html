Comprehensive English Learning Website

With the ambition to build a comprehensive platform for English enthusiasts to develop their skills, we have created this website. Following the criteria of "continuous exercises for continuous progress" this website is constructed with a database of over 4,000 exercises distributed across 6 skill types (Listening, Speaking, Reading, Writing, Vocabulary, Grammar). The database continues to grow each day. Data is collected from various sources, with over 50% of it being generated by AI tools. Therefore, the website may not be completely free of errors. If you discover any mistakes, please contact us via email at 3000vocab@gmail.com so that we can make corrections as quickly as possible.

Unlike typical English learning websites, our platform is entirely free and always will be!

1. Listening

It consists of four exercise types: Listening to music, dialogues, short sentences, and podcasts. Generally, podcasts are likely the most challenging exercise type, but they also have the greatest potential to accelerate your listening skills because you must listen and understand the content in detail. However, do not be disheartened or discouraged if you cannot understand podcasts initially. Try practicing with the other three types to gradually familiarize your ears with English.

2. Speaking

We cannot directly evaluate your speaking ability due to website limitations. However, we provide materials for you to practice this skill. Speaking includes numerous questions and sample answers. The recommended method is "Shadowing." "Shadowing" is a widely-used speaking skill in English that requires you to mimic the speech of a native speaker, including pronunciation and intonation. It is considered the fastest way to improve speaking fluency. Try imitating the sample answers repeatedly until you memorize them and can speak fluently, just like the provided model passage. After successfully doing so, write your own answers and practice with them.

3. Reading

Reading comprises various passages covering diverse topics. The exercise types primarily include fill-in-the-blanks, True-False-NotGiven, finding information in the passage, and multiple-choice questions. These are common exercise formats in exams like IELTS, TOEIC, and TOEFL. If you are aware of other exercise types, please provide feedback so we can consider adding them if appropriate.

4. Writing

Firstly, we regret to inform you that the writing feature, including score assessment and automatic error correction, is temporarily disabled due to copyright issues. Specifically, we were using a third-party API that required a fee. In the future, we will strive to reinstate these two features. For now, you can only write and save your essays. With over 100 essay prompts divided into 5 main groups, these are real exam essays collected from actual IELTS exams. Additionally, for each essay prompt, we provide two high-quality sample essays for your reference and use.

5. Vocabulary

Oh, did you know? This website is called 3000vocab.com, and it was originally built with the primary purpose of practicing and improving vocabulary. This is the website's core feature. With the vocabulary learning method based on phrases and repetition ("Repetition" method), you will naturally and effectively memorize vocabulary. The website includes approximately 3000 words divided into various scientifically and logically categorized groups.

6. Quiz

If you want to improve your grammar or prepare for exams like TOEIC and TOEFL, this exercise type is indispensable.
